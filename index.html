AQuA-RAT Overview

Our dataset is collected by using defining a new representation language to annotate over the AQuA-RAT dataset. AQuA-RAT provids math questions and their options, rationale, and the correct option.

Question: A train running at the speed of 48 km / hr crosses a pole in 9 seconds . what is the length of the train ?
Rationale: Speed = ( 48 x 5 / 18 ) m / sec = ( 40 / 3 ) m / sec . length of the train = ( speed x time ) . length of the train = ( 40 / 3 x 9 ) m = 120 m . answer is c .
Options: a ) 140 , b ) 130 , c ) 120 , d ) 170 , e ) 160
Correct Option is: C

The rationales are noisy, incomplete and sometimes incorrect. We correct these rationales by providing stepwise solutions for a portion of AQuA-RAT.
Our Annotated Formula: multiply(divide(multiply(48, const_1000), const_3600), 9)

Annotation Scheme Explanation

We have designed an annotation platform throughFigure Eight. We have defined a representation language that can capture the steps necessary for finding the solution. Each step in our final program is a predefined operation and its arguments, which can be chosen from the problem text, a list of constants or previous calculations. Here is the overview of our job:

  • Problem text is shown to the contributors.
  • Based on the category of the problem, related operations are provided for the contributor.
  • Contributors can see operations' hint containing the formula, argument and explanation by hovering over operations.
  • After selecting an operation, a list of valid arguments are shown to the contributor to select from. When the arguments list is complete, our program calculates the output value of the operation and adds it to valid argument list.
  • This process will continue until the operation values are close to the final answer by a small error margin.
  • We record the contributor’s actions in the form of a line of math expressions.

Figure eight provides unique features that help us collect data that is clean, reliable and accurate. The task of annotating math word problem is especially complicated and time consuming. The points that should considered are :

  • Accuracy of the solution : To ensure accuracy, we have defined several test questions which will be shown to contributors randomly. They should maintain an accuracy above specific threshold. We constantly monitored contributors’ answers to quality questions to allow different variations of the solution.
  • Submitting a program : Since we have a final correct answer from AQuA-RAT we can check the calculation result of a contributor-provided program. The program can only be submitted if the final answer matches the correct answer and at least one value from problem is used in the program.

Validation Scheme Explanation

The results of the annotation tasks are not all correct. There are cases that a logically wrong program can get to the final answer. Therefore we designed a validation task. Here are the steps for that task:

  • We show the following to the contributor: The problem, the rationale, and the math expression that has been annotated as the solution.
  • By clicking over every part of the expression they can see the calculation result. They can compare the numbers with the numbers form the rationale.
  • If the annotation is correct and aligned with rationale they should choose “yes” ; Otherwise they should choose “no”.

The quality of the validation task is also maintained by designing the quality questions.
